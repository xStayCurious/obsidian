-----------------------------------
802.11 STANDARDS
-----------------------------------

The 802.11 standards are:
- protocols for implementing wireless LAN (WLAN) communication

They define:
- wireless networking operation
- frequency usage
- speed capabilities
- wireless communication standards

-----------------------------------
PURPOSE OF 802.11
-----------------------------------

802.11 standards provide:
- wireless communication over LANs
- standardized wireless networking
- compatibility between wireless devices

-----------------------------------
KEY IMPROVEMENTS OVER TIME
-----------------------------------

Each newer 802.11 standard improves:
- speed
- range
- reliability
- efficiency

-----------------------------------
802.11a
-----------------------------------

802.11a:
- operates in the 5 GHz band
- maximum speed of 54 Mbps

-----------------------------------
802.11a CHARACTERISTICS
-----------------------------------

Advantages:
- less interference

Disadvantages:
- shorter range compared to 2.4 GHz standards

-----------------------------------
802.11b
-----------------------------------

802.11b:
- operates in the 2.4 GHz band
- speeds up to 11 Mbps

-----------------------------------
802.11b CHARACTERISTICS
-----------------------------------

Advantages:
- longer range
- better obstacle penetration

Disadvantages:
- more susceptible to interference

-----------------------------------
802.11g
-----------------------------------

802.11g:
- operates in the 2.4 GHz band
- speeds up to 54 Mbps

-----------------------------------
802.11g CHARACTERISTICS
-----------------------------------

- combines benefits of 802.11a and 802.11b
- backward compatible with 802.11b

-----------------------------------
802.11n (WiFi 4)
-----------------------------------

802.11n:
- also called WiFi 4
- speeds up to 600 Mbps
- operates on both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands

-----------------------------------
802.11n FEATURES
-----------------------------------

Uses:
- MIMO (Multiple Input Multiple Output)

Benefits:
- improved speed
- improved range

-----------------------------------
802.11ac (WiFi 5)
-----------------------------------

802.11ac:
- also called WiFi 5
- operates only on 5 GHz
- theoretical maximum speed of 3.46 Gbps

-----------------------------------
802.11ac FEATURES
-----------------------------------

Uses:
- wider channels
- more spatial streams
- higher modulation

-----------------------------------
802.11ac BENEFITS
-----------------------------------

Ideal for:
- high-definition streaming
- high-speed data transfer

-----------------------------------
802.11ax (WiFi 6)
-----------------------------------

802.11ax:
- also called WiFi 6
- theoretical maximum speed of 9.6 Gbps
- operates on both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands

-----------------------------------
802.11ax GOALS
-----------------------------------

Designed to improve:
- WLAN efficiency
- crowded wireless environments
- device support
- performance

-----------------------------------
802.11ax FEATURES
-----------------------------------

Introduces:
- OFDMA
- BSS Coloring

-----------------------------------
802.11ax BENEFITS
-----------------------------------

Benefits:
- higher speeds
- better coverage
- reduced interference
- reduced power consumption

-----------------------------------
802.11ax USE CASES
-----------------------------------

Designed for:
- 4K/8K streaming
- virtual reality
- IoT devices
- high-density wireless environments

-----------------------------------
2.4 GHz VS 5 GHz
-----------------------------------

2.4 GHz:
- better range
- better obstacle penetration
- more interference

5 GHz:
- faster speeds
- less interference
- shorter range

-----------------------------------
MIMO
-----------------------------------

MIMO =
Multiple Input Multiple Output

Used in:
- 802.11n

Purpose:
- improve wireless speed and range

-----------------------------------
OFDMA
-----------------------------------

OFDMA:
- introduced in WiFi 6

Purpose:
- improve efficiency
- support many devices simultaneously

{{{
Additional Context:
OFDMA allows a wireless channel to be divided into smaller sub-channels so multiple devices can transmit simultaneously.

Source:
Cisco Wi-Fi 6 Overview
https://www.cisco.com/c/en/us/products/wireless/what-is-wifi-6.html
}}}

-----------------------------------
BSS COLORING
-----------------------------------

BSS Coloring:
- introduced in WiFi 6

Purpose:
- reduce wireless interference in crowded environments

{{{
Additional Context:
BSS Coloring identifies overlapping wireless networks to help devices ignore unrelated wireless traffic and improve efficiency.

Source:
Aruba Networks Wi-Fi 6 Guide
https://www.arubanetworks.com/faq/what-is-bss-coloring/
}}}

COMMON EXAM SCENARIOS
-----------------------------------

Scenario:
"A wireless network operates on both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz and uses MIMO."

Answer:
802.11n

-----------------------------------

Scenario:
"A wireless standard operates only on 5 GHz and supports gigabit speeds."

Answer:
802.11ac

-----------------------------------

Scenario:
"A wireless standard improves efficiency in crowded environments using OFDMA and BSS Coloring."

Answer:
802.11ax

-----------------------------------

Scenario:
"A wireless standard operates on 2.4 GHz and supports 11 Mbps."

Answer:
802.11b

-----------------------------------
COMMON EXAM TRAPS
-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking all standards use both frequency bands.

Wrong.

802.11a:
- only 5 GHz

802.11ac:
- only 5 GHz

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Confusing WiFi 5 and WiFi 6.

WiFi 5:
- 802.11ac

WiFi 6:
- 802.11ax

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking 2.4 GHz is always better.

Wrong.

2.4 GHz:
- better range
- more interference

5 GHz:
- higher speed
- less interference
