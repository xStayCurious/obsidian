-----------------------------------
FIBER OPTIC CABLE
-----------------------------------

Fiber optic cable:
- transmits data using light signals
through:
- thin strands of glass or plastic

-----------------------------------
CORE PURPOSE
-----------------------------------

Fiber optic cabling is designed for:
- high-speed data transmission
- long-distance communication
- high-bandwidth networking

-----------------------------------
HOW FIBER WORKS
-----------------------------------

Fiber uses:
- light pulses
instead of:
- electrical signals

-----------------------------------
FIBER CHARACTERISTICS
-----------------------------------

Fiber optic cable provides:
- high bandwidth
- high speed
- long-distance capability
- resistance to electromagnetic interference (EMI)

-----------------------------------
EMI RESISTANCE
-----------------------------------

Fiber optic cable:
- is immune to electromagnetic interference

This is because:
- it uses light instead of electricity

-----------------------------------
WHY EMI RESISTANCE MATTERS
-----------------------------------

Fiber is useful in environments with:
- electrical interference
- industrial equipment
- high electromagnetic activity

-----------------------------------
LONG-DISTANCE COMMUNICATION
-----------------------------------

Fiber supports:
- much longer cable distances
than:
- copper cabling

-----------------------------------
HIGH BANDWIDTH
-----------------------------------

Fiber optic cable supports:
- very high data transmission rates

-----------------------------------
FIBER TYPES
-----------------------------------

The guide lists:
- Single-mode fiber (SMF)
- Multimode fiber (MMF)

-----------------------------------
SINGLE-MODE FIBER (SMF)
-----------------------------------

Single-mode fiber:
- uses a very small core
- allows only one light mode/path
- designed for long-distance communication

-----------------------------------
SMF CHARACTERISTICS
-----------------------------------

- Longer distance support
- Higher bandwidth capability
- More precise light transmission

-----------------------------------
SMF LIGHT SOURCE
-----------------------------------

Single-mode fiber commonly uses:
- lasers

-----------------------------------
MULTIMODE FIBER (MMF)
-----------------------------------

Multimode fiber:
- uses a larger core
- allows multiple light paths/modes
- designed for shorter distances

-----------------------------------
MMF CHARACTERISTICS
-----------------------------------

- Shorter distance support
- Lower cost than SMF
- Easier light injection

-----------------------------------
MMF LIGHT SOURCE
-----------------------------------

Multimode fiber commonly uses:
- LEDs

-----------------------------------
SMF VS MMF
-----------------------------------

SMF:
- longer distance
- higher bandwidth
- laser-based
- smaller core

MMF:
- shorter distance
- lower cost
- LED-based
- larger core

-----------------------------------
FIBER CONNECTORS
-----------------------------------

The guide lists several fiber connectors:
- LC
- ST
- SC

-----------------------------------
LC CONNECTOR
-----------------------------------

LC:
- small form-factor connector
- commonly used in modern networking

-----------------------------------
ST CONNECTOR
-----------------------------------

ST:
- uses a twist-lock mechanism

-----------------------------------
SC CONNECTOR
-----------------------------------

SC:
- square-shaped connector
- push-pull design

-----------------------------------
FIBER ADVANTAGES
-----------------------------------

Advantages of fiber:
- high speed
- high bandwidth
- long distances
- EMI resistance
- reliable transmission

-----------------------------------
FIBER DISADVANTAGES
-----------------------------------

The guide notes:
- fiber can be more expensive
- installation may be more difficult

-----------------------------------
COMMON FIBER USE CASES
-----------------------------------

Fiber is commonly used for:
- backbone connections
- long-distance networking
- enterprise networking
- high-speed internet

-----------------------------------
BACKBONE NETWORKS
-----------------------------------

Fiber is ideal for:
- network backbones

because:
- it supports high throughput and long distances

-----------------------------------
WIRELESS VS WIRED
-----------------------------------

Fiber is:
- wired transmission media
