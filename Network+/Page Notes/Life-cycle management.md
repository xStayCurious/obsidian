-----------------------------------
LIFE-CYCLE MANAGEMENT
-----------------------------------

Life-cycle management refers to:
- managing technology from deployment
through:
- operation
- maintenance
- retirement

-----------------------------------
CORE PURPOSE
-----------------------------------

Life-cycle management helps organizations:
- maintain stability
- reduce security risks
- control costs
- improve reliability
- plan upgrades
- avoid unsupported systems

-----------------------------------
MAIN EXAM TOPICS
-----------------------------------

The guide specifically covers:
- EOL
- EOS
- software management
- decommissioning

-----------------------------------
EOL
-----------------------------------

EOL =
End of Life

-----------------------------------
EOL PURPOSE
-----------------------------------

EOL indicates:
- a product is no longer actively sold
or:
- no longer actively developed

-----------------------------------
WHAT EOL MEANS
-----------------------------------

When hardware/software reaches EOL:
- vendors may stop feature development
- vendors may stop new sales
- replacement planning becomes important

-----------------------------------
EOL RISKS
-----------------------------------

EOL systems may eventually:
- become unsupported
- become insecure
- become incompatible
- become difficult to maintain

-----------------------------------
EOL EXAM IDEA
-----------------------------------

EOL generally means:
- the product lifecycle is ending

-----------------------------------
EOS
-----------------------------------

EOS =
End of Support

-----------------------------------
EOS PURPOSE
-----------------------------------

EOS indicates:
- vendor support has ended

-----------------------------------
WHAT EOS MEANS
-----------------------------------

After EOS:
- patches may stop
- updates may stop
- technical support may stop
- security fixes may stop

-----------------------------------
EOS RISKS
-----------------------------------

EOS systems may become:
- security liabilities
- compliance risks
- operational risks

-----------------------------------
EOL VS EOS
-----------------------------------

EOL:
- product lifecycle ending
- sales/development stopping

EOS:
- vendor support stopping

-----------------------------------
IMPORTANT EXAM DISTINCTION
-----------------------------------

A product may be:
- EOL
before:
- EOS

Meaning:
- not sold anymore
but:
- still temporarily supported

-----------------------------------
UNSUPPORTED SYSTEM RISKS
-----------------------------------

Unsupported systems increase risk of:
- vulnerabilities
- malware compromise
- incompatibility
- downtime

COMMON EXAM TRAPS
-----------------------------------

Trap:
Confusing EOL and EOS.

EOL:
- product lifecycle ending

EOS:
- support ending

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking EOL means support already ended.

Wrong.

A product may still receive support after EOL.

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking patch management only adds features.

Wrong.

Patching commonly:
- fixes vulnerabilities
- improves stability

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking deleting files securely sanitizes drives.

Wrong.

Deleted files may still be recoverable.

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking firmware and drivers are identical.

Wrong.

Firmware:
- embedded low-level device software

Drivers:
- OS-level communication software

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking decommissioning only means unplugging equipment.

Wrong.

Proper decommissioning includes:
- sanitization
- inventory updates
- disposal procedures
- access removal
