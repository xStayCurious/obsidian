AUDITS AND COMPLIANCE
-----------------------------------

Audits and compliance involve:
- verifying organizations follow security policies
- meeting legal/regulatory requirements
- protecting sensitive information

-----------------------------------
WHY COMPLIANCE MATTERS
-----------------------------------

Organizations may face:
- legal penalties
- financial penalties
- lawsuits
- reputational damage

if compliance requirements are not met

-----------------------------------
AUDITS
-----------------------------------

Audits evaluate:
- security controls
- configurations
- policies
- operational practices

-----------------------------------
AUDIT PURPOSE
-----------------------------------

Audits help verify:
- compliance
- effectiveness of controls
- adherence to standards

-----------------------------------
INTERNAL AUDITS
-----------------------------------

Internal audits are performed by:
- the organization itself

-----------------------------------
EXTERNAL AUDITS
-----------------------------------

External audits are performed by:
- third parties
- regulators
- independent assessors

-----------------------------------
AUDIT EVIDENCE
-----------------------------------

Audits commonly review:
- logs
- configurations
- policies
- access records
- security controls

-----------------------------------
COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORKS
-----------------------------------

Compliance frameworks define:
- security/control requirements

-----------------------------------
COMMON COMPLIANCE GOALS
-----------------------------------

Goals include:
- protecting customer data
- ensuring privacy
- reducing risk
- standardizing security

===================================
DATA LOCALITY
===================================

-----------------------------------
DATA LOCALITY
-----------------------------------

Data locality refers to:
- where data is physically/geographically stored

-----------------------------------
CORE PURPOSE
-----------------------------------

Data locality helps organizations:
- comply with laws/regulations
- control jurisdictional exposure
- manage privacy requirements

-----------------------------------
WHY DATA LOCALITY MATTERS
-----------------------------------

Different countries may have:
- different privacy laws
- different data access rules
- different compliance requirements

-----------------------------------
CLOUD COMPUTING
-----------------------------------

Data locality is especially important in:
- cloud environments

-----------------------------------
CLOUD RISKS
-----------------------------------

Cloud providers may store data:
- across multiple regions/countries

-----------------------------------
REGIONAL RESTRICTIONS
-----------------------------------

Organizations may require:
- data remain within certain countries/regions

-----------------------------------
DATA SOVEREIGNTY
-----------------------------------

{{{
Additional Context:
Data sovereignty refers to data being subject to the laws of the country where it is stored.

Source:
Microsoft Data Residency Documentation
https://learn.microsoft.com/
}}}

-----------------------------------
COMPLIANCE DRIVERS
-----------------------------------

Data locality may be required by:
- privacy laws
- government regulations
- industry requirements

-----------------------------------
DATA REPLICATION
-----------------------------------

Cloud providers may replicate data:
- across geographic locations

-----------------------------------
WHY REPLICATION MATTERS
-----------------------------------

Replication may affect:
- compliance requirements
- privacy obligations

-----------------------------------
COMMON DATA LOCALITY CONCERNS
-----------------------------------

Examples:
- customer privacy
- government access
- regulatory jurisdiction
- cross-border transfers

-----------------------------------
HIGH-YIELD EXAM IDEA
-----------------------------------

Important concept:
Organizations may need to know:
- exactly where data is stored

===================================
PCI DSS
===================================

-----------------------------------
PCI DSS
-----------------------------------

PCI DSS =
Payment Card Industry
Data Security Standard

-----------------------------------
CORE PURPOSE
-----------------------------------

PCI DSS protects:
- payment card information

-----------------------------------
WHY PCI DSS EXISTS
-----------------------------------

Organizations handling cardholder data are:
- major attack targets

-----------------------------------
WHO PCI DSS APPLIES TO
-----------------------------------

PCI DSS applies to organizations that:
- process
- store
- transmit

payment card data

-----------------------------------
CARDHOLDER DATA
-----------------------------------

Examples:
- credit card numbers
- payment information
- card security data

-----------------------------------
PCI DSS GOALS
-----------------------------------

PCI DSS aims to:
- reduce payment fraud
- secure cardholder data
- standardize payment security

-----------------------------------
COMMON PCI DSS REQUIREMENTS
-----------------------------------

Examples:
- encryption
- firewalls
- access controls
- logging
- vulnerability management

-----------------------------------
ACCESS CONTROL
-----------------------------------

PCI DSS emphasizes:
- least privilege
- restricted access

-----------------------------------
ENCRYPTION
-----------------------------------

PCI DSS commonly requires:
- encryption of sensitive payment data

-----------------------------------
LOGGING
-----------------------------------

PCI DSS requires:
- logging/monitoring activity

-----------------------------------
VULNERABILITY MANAGEMENT
-----------------------------------

Organizations commonly must:
- patch systems
- scan for vulnerabilities

-----------------------------------
SEGMENTATION
-----------------------------------

{{{
Additional Context:
Network segmentation may reduce PCI DSS scope.

Source:
PCI Security Standards Council
https://www.pcisecuritystandards.org/
}}}

-----------------------------------
PCI DSS NONCOMPLIANCE
-----------------------------------

Noncompliance may lead to:
- fines
- liability
- reputational damage
- loss of payment processing privileges

-----------------------------------
COMMON PCI DSS CONTROLS
-----------------------------------

Examples:
- MFA
- strong passwords
- encryption
- monitoring
- IDS/IPS

-----------------------------------
THIRD-PARTY COMPLIANCE
-----------------------------------

Organizations may use:
- external PCI assessors

-----------------------------------
SAQ
-----------------------------------

{{{
Additional Context:

Self-Assessment Questionnaire

Some organizations self-assess PCI compliance.

===================================
GDPR
===================================

-----------------------------------
GDPR
-----------------------------------

GDPR =
General Data Protection Regulation

-----------------------------------
CORE PURPOSE
-----------------------------------

GDPR protects:
- personal privacy/data

-----------------------------------
WHY GDPR EXISTS
-----------------------------------

Organizations increasingly collect:
- personal information
- behavioral data
- online activity data

GDPR establishes:
- privacy/security requirements

-----------------------------------
GDPR REGION
-----------------------------------

GDPR is associated with:
- the European Union (EU)

-----------------------------------
WHO GDPR APPLIES TO
-----------------------------------

GDPR may apply to organizations handling:
- EU resident data

even if the organization is:
- outside the EU

-----------------------------------
PERSONAL DATA
-----------------------------------

Examples:
- names
- email addresses
- IP addresses
- location data
- identifiers

-----------------------------------
GDPR GOALS
-----------------------------------

GDPR aims to:
- improve privacy rights
- increase transparency
- protect personal information

-----------------------------------
CONSENT
-----------------------------------

GDPR emphasizes:
- informed consent

-----------------------------------
USER RIGHTS
-----------------------------------

GDPR provides users rights such as:
- access to personal data
- deletion requests
- correction requests

-----------------------------------
RIGHT TO BE FORGOTTEN
-----------------------------------

{{{
Additional Context:
GDPR includes the "right to erasure" ("right to be forgotten").

Source:
European Commission GDPR Guidance
https://commission.europa.eu/
}}}

-----------------------------------
DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION
-----------------------------------

GDPR may require:
- rapid breach notification

-----------------------------------
DATA MINIMIZATION
-----------------------------------

GDPR encourages:
- collecting only necessary data

-----------------------------------
PRIVACY BY DESIGN
-----------------------------------

{{{
Additional Context:
GDPR encourages integrating privacy protections into systems/processes from the beginning.

Source:
European Commission GDPR Guidance
https://commission.europa.eu/
}}}

-----------------------------------
ENCRYPTION
-----------------------------------

Encryption helps support:
- GDPR compliance

-----------------------------------
ACCESS CONTROL
-----------------------------------

GDPR emphasizes:
- protection against unauthorized access

-----------------------------------
GDPR PENALTIES
-----------------------------------

Violations may result in:
- severe financial penalties

-----------------------------------
DATA TRANSFER RULES
-----------------------------------

GDPR regulates:
- cross-border data transfers

-----------------------------------
DATA LOCALITY RELATIONSHIP
-----------------------------------

GDPR strongly relates to:
- data locality
- data sovereignty

-----------------------------------

Very important exam facts:

- Audits verify compliance/security controls
- Data locality = where data is stored

- PCI DSS applies to payment processing environments

- Data locality is important in cloud computing

COMMON EXAM TRAPS
-----------------------------------

Trap:
Confusing PCI DSS and GDPR.

PCI DSS:
- payment card security

GDPR:
- privacy/personal data protection

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking GDPR applies only to EU companies.

Wrong.

GDPR may apply to:
- organizations processing EU resident data

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking audits only happen after incidents.

Wrong.

Audits are commonly:
- routine/compliance activities

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking data locality only matters for cloud providers.

Wrong.

It matters for:
- any organization storing regulated data

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking encryption alone guarantees compliance.

Wrong.

Compliance also requires:
- policies
- monitoring
- access controls
- auditing

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking PCI DSS only applies to banks.

Wrong.

PCI DSS applies broadly to:
- organizations processing card payments
