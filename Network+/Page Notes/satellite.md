SATELLITE COMMUNICATION
-----------------------------------

Satellite communication:
- uses satellites orbiting the Earth
to:
- relay data
- relay voice
- relay video

across:
- long distances

-----------------------------------
CORE PURPOSE
-----------------------------------

Satellite communication provides:
- long-distance connectivity
- broadband internet access
- communication in remote areas

-----------------------------------
GLOBAL COVERAGE
-----------------------------------

Satellite communication offers:
- global coverage

It can provide connectivity:
- where traditional infrastructure may not exist

-----------------------------------
REMOTE AREA SUPPORT
-----------------------------------

Satellite communication is useful in:
- rural areas
- remote regions
- locations without wired infrastructure

-----------------------------------
HOW SATELLITE COMMUNICATION WORKS
-----------------------------------

Data is transmitted:
- from Earth
to:
- a satellite

The satellite:
- relays the communication
back:
- to another location on Earth

-----------------------------------
SATELLITE DISH
-----------------------------------

Users typically communicate with satellites using:
- a satellite dish

-----------------------------------

Satellite communication can provide:
- broadband internet access

- data

-----------------------------------
LATENCY
-----------------------------------

The guide states:
- satellite communication can experience latency issues

-----------------------------------
WHY LATENCY OCCURS
-----------------------------------

Latency occurs because:
- signals must travel large distances
between:
- Earth
and:
- orbiting satellites

{{{
Additional Context:
Higher latency is especially common with geostationary satellites because signals travel tens of thousands of kilometers to orbit and back.

Source:
Federal Communications Commission (FCC)
https://www.fcc.gov/general/satellite
}}}
