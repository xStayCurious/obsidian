COMMON EXAM TRAPS
-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking cybersecurity alone protects infrastructure.

Wrong.

Physical access may bypass:
- logical security controls

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Confusing tailgating and piggybacking.

Tailgating:
- unauthorized following

Piggybacking:
- authorized person intentionally assists

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking cameras prevent attacks automatically.

Wrong.

Cameras primarily:
- monitor
- deter
- record

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking fail-open and fail-closed are identical.

Fail-open:
- unlocks on power loss

Fail-closed:
- stays locked on power loss

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking badge systems eliminate need for logging.

Wrong.

Logging remains critical for:
- auditing
- investigations

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking IP cameras are isolated from cybersecurity concerns.

Wrong.

IP cameras are:
- network-connected devices

HIGH-YIELD FLASHCARDS
-----------------------------------

Q:
What is the purpose of physical security?

A:
Protect facilities and infrastructure from physical threats.

-----------------------------------

Q:
What are security cameras used for?

A:
Monitoring, deterrence, and recording activity.

-----------------------------------

Q:
What is an IP camera?

A:
A network-connected security camera.

-----------------------------------

Q:
What does NVR stand for?

A:
Network Video Recorder.

-----------------------------------

Q:
What is the purpose of locks?

A:
Restrict physical access.

-----------------------------------

Q:
What is tailgating?

A:
Unauthorized following into a secure area.

-----------------------------------

Q:
What is a mantrap?

A:
A controlled entry area limiting one-person access.

-----------------------------------

Q:
What is the difference between fail-open and fail-closed locks?

A:
Fail-open unlocks during power failure; fail-closed remains locked.

-----------------------------------

Q:
Why are access logs important?

A:
Support auditing and investigations.

-----------------------------------

Q:
Why should IP cameras be secured?

A:
They are network-connected devices and may become attack targets.
