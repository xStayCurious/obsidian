-----------------------------------
FREQUENCY OPTIONS
-----------------------------------

Wireless frequency options refer to:
- the radio frequency bands used for wireless communication

The guide identifies:
- 2.4 GHz
- 5 GHz
- 6 GHz

-----------------------------------
CORE PURPOSE
-----------------------------------

Different wireless frequency bands provide different tradeoffs involving:
- range
- speed
- interference
- capacity
- device compatibility

-----------------------------------
WHY MULTIPLE FREQUENCY BANDS EXIST
-----------------------------------

No single wireless frequency band is ideal for every situation.

Some prioritize:
- coverage and penetration

Others prioritize:
- throughput and capacity

-----------------------------------
2.4 GHz BAND
-----------------------------------

2.4 GHz is:
- one of the oldest and most widely supported WiFi frequency bands

-----------------------------------
2.4 GHz ADVANTAGES
-----------------------------------

Advantages:
- longer range
- better wall penetration
- broad device compatibility
- useful over larger areas

-----------------------------------
WHY 2.4 GHz HAS BETTER RANGE
-----------------------------------

Lower frequencies generally:
- travel farther
- penetrate obstacles more effectively

Because 2.4 GHz is lower frequency than 5 GHz or 6 GHz:
- it typically performs better through walls and objects

-----------------------------------
2.4 GHz DISADVANTAGES
-----------------------------------

Disadvantages:
- more congestion
- more interference
- fewer non-overlapping channels
- lower throughput potential

-----------------------------------
COMMON 2.4 GHz INTERFERENCE SOURCES
-----------------------------------

The 2.4 GHz band is heavily used by:
- microwaves
- Bluetooth devices
- cordless phones
- neighboring WiFi
- IoT devices

-----------------------------------
2.4 GHz EXAM IDEA
-----------------------------------

2.4 GHz is generally preferred when:
- coverage matters more than speed

-----------------------------------
2.4 GHz USE CASES
-----------------------------------

Good for:
- large homes
- long-range coverage
- IoT devices
- devices far from the AP
- obstacle-heavy environments

-----------------------------------
2.4 GHz CHANNELS
-----------------------------------

The most important Network+ fact:
The commonly used non-overlapping channels in 2.4 GHz are:
- 1
- 6
- 11

-----------------------------------
WHY 2.4 GHz IS CONGESTED
-----------------------------------

2.4 GHz has:
- limited spectrum
- overlapping channels
- many competing devices

This often leads to:
- slower performance
- interference
- retransmissions

-----------------------------------
5 GHz BAND
-----------------------------------

5 GHz is:
- a higher-frequency WiFi band
offering:
- higher throughput
- lower congestion
- more channels

-----------------------------------
5 GHz ADVANTAGES
-----------------------------------

Advantages:
- faster speeds
- more non-overlapping channels
- reduced interference
- better support for dense deployments

-----------------------------------
WHY 5 GHz SUPPORTS HIGHER SPEEDS
-----------------------------------

5 GHz has:
- more available spectrum
- wider channel support
- more channel availability

This allows:
- higher throughput potential

-----------------------------------
5 GHz DISADVANTAGES
-----------------------------------

Disadvantages:
- shorter range
- weaker wall penetration
- reduced coverage compared to 2.4 GHz

-----------------------------------
WHY 5 GHz HAS SHORTER RANGE
-----------------------------------

Higher frequencies:
- attenuate more quickly
- penetrate walls less effectively

-----------------------------------
5 GHz EXAM IDEA
-----------------------------------

5 GHz is generally preferred when:
- speed matters more than range

-----------------------------------
5 GHz USE CASES
-----------------------------------

Good for:
- streaming
- gaming
- high-density offices
- enterprise deployments
- high-bandwidth applications

-----------------------------------
5 GHz CHANNEL AVAILABILITY
-----------------------------------

5 GHz provides:
- many more non-overlapping channels than 2.4 GHz

This improves:
- scalability
- interference reduction

-----------------------------------
DFS CHANNELS
-----------------------------------

DFS =
Dynamic Frequency Selection

DFS is strongly associated with:
- 5 GHz wireless networks

-----------------------------------
DFS PURPOSE
-----------------------------------

DFS allows APs to:
- detect radar systems
- avoid interfering with radar
- automatically switch channels if radar is detected

-----------------------------------
DFS EXAM IDEA
-----------------------------------

If a wireless AP changes channels because radar is detected:
- DFS is involved

-----------------------------------
802.11h
-----------------------------------

The guide associates 802.11h with:
- DFS
- TPC
- regulatory compliance

-----------------------------------
TPC
-----------------------------------

TPC =
Transmit Power Control

TPC adjusts:
- wireless transmission power

Purpose:
- reduce interference
- improve RF efficiency
- comply with regulations

-----------------------------------
6 GHz BAND
-----------------------------------

6 GHz is:
- the newest major WiFi frequency band listed in the guide

Strongly associated with:
- WiFi 6E

-----------------------------------
6 GHz ADVANTAGES
-----------------------------------

Advantages:
- significantly more spectrum
- reduced congestion
- higher capacity
- lower latency
- support for more simultaneous devices

-----------------------------------
WHY 6 GHz IS IMPORTANT
-----------------------------------

Older bands became crowded due to:
- growing wireless usage
- many client devices
- IoT growth
- dense deployments

6 GHz adds:
- substantial new wireless spectrum

-----------------------------------
6 GHz PERFORMANCE BENEFITS
-----------------------------------

6 GHz is excellent for:
- dense enterprise deployments
- modern high-speed wireless
- low-latency applications
- high-throughput environments

-----------------------------------
6 GHz DISADVANTAGES
-----------------------------------

Disadvantages:
- shortest range of the major WiFi bands
- weakest wall penetration
- requires compatible hardware

-----------------------------------
6 GHz DEVICE REQUIREMENTS
-----------------------------------

Devices must support:
- WiFi 6E/6 GHz operation

Older WiFi devices:
- cannot use 6 GHz

-----------------------------------
BAND STEERING
-----------------------------------

Band steering automatically encourages compatible clients to move to:
- 5 GHz
or:
- 6 GHz

instead of:
- congested 2.4 GHz

-----------------------------------
WHY BAND STEERING EXISTS
-----------------------------------

Band steering helps:
- reduce 2.4 GHz congestion
- improve throughput
- balance wireless load
- optimize client performance

-----------------------------------
RANGE VS SPEED TRADEOFF
-----------------------------------

2.4 GHz:
- best range
- more interference
- lower speed potential

5 GHz:
- balanced speed/capacity
- shorter range
- less congestion

6 GHz:
- highest capacity/cleanest spectrum
- shortest range
- newest technology

-----------------------------------
WIRELESS PENETRATION
-----------------------------------

Penetration refers to:
- how well wireless signals pass through obstacles

Generally:
- lower frequencies penetrate better

-----------------------------------
OBSTACLE EFFECTS
-----------------------------------

Walls, floors, metal, and concrete can reduce:
- wireless signal strength

Higher-frequency bands:
- are more affected by obstacles

-----------------------------------
DENSE DEPLOYMENTS
-----------------------------------

Dense environments include:
- offices
- stadiums
- apartments
- schools
- airports

These environments often benefit from:
- 5 GHz
- 6 GHz

because they support:
- more non-overlapping channels
- more capacity

-----------------------------------
LEGACY DEVICE SUPPORT
-----------------------------------

Older devices often support:
- 2.4 GHz only

Modern devices commonly support:
- dual-band operation
or:
- tri-band operation

-----------------------------------
DUAL-BAND
-----------------------------------

Dual-band devices support:
- 2.4 GHz
and:
- 5 GHz

-----------------------------------
TRI-BAND
-----------------------------------

Tri-band devices may support:
- 2.4 GHz
- 5 GHz
- 6 GHz

-----------------------------------
WIRELESS PERFORMANCE FACTORS
-----------------------------------

Wireless performance depends on:
- frequency band
- interference
- AP placement
- channel width
- obstacles
- client density
- antenna design

-----------------------------------
HIGH-YIELD NETWORK+ FACTS
-----------------------------------

Very important exam facts:

- 2.4 GHz = better range
- 5 GHz = higher throughput
- 6 GHz = newest/highest capacity
- 2.4 GHz is more congested
- 5 GHz has more channels
- 6 GHz requires compatible devices
- DFS protects radar systems
- Band steering moves clients away from congested bands

-----------------------------------

2.4 GHz

-----------------------------------

Scenario:
"A company needs higher wireless throughput with less interference."

Best answer:
5 GHz

-----------------------------------

Scenario:
"A dense enterprise deployment needs maximum capacity and lower latency."

Best answer:
6 GHz

-----------------------------------

Scenario:
"A wireless AP changes channels because radar is detected."

Answer:
DFS

-----------------------------------

Scenario:
"A company wants clients automatically moved from congested 2.4 GHz."

Answer:
Band steering

-----------------------------------

Scenario:
"A deployment needs the best support for older wireless devices."

Answer:
2.4 GHz

-----------------------------------

Scenario:
"A modern deployment wants cleaner RF spectrum and WiFi 6E."

Answer:
6 GHz

-----------------------------------
COMMON EXAM TRAPS
-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking 5 GHz is always better.

Wrong.

5 GHz:
- faster
but:
- shorter range

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking 2.4 GHz is obsolete.

Wrong.

2.4 GHz:
- still valuable for coverage and compatibility

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking 6 GHz works with all WiFi devices.

Wrong.

Devices must support:
- 6 GHz/WiFi 6E

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking higher frequencies penetrate walls better.

Wrong.

Lower frequencies generally penetrate better.

-----------------------------------

Trap:
Thinking DFS improves speed.

Wrong.

DFS primarily:
- avoids radar interference

-----------------------------------

-----------------------------------
